The Coastal Wonders of the Eastern Shores

Discover the enchanting Tidewater region of North Carolina, where the waters of the Atlantic Ocean meet the vast, fertile plains that stretch deep into the heart of the state. This area, also known as the Coastal Plain, is a beautiful combination of scenic beaches, diverse ecosystems, and significant historical landmarks. The Tidewater is not just a destination; it is a journey where maritime heritage flourishes and natural beauty thrives. This region is a sanctuary for those who seek the quiet serenity of coastal ecosystems and the spirited adventures of seaside towns. Engage with the Tidewater's enduring legacy, where every corner holds a story and every sunset brings a promise of discovery.

Location: Outer Banks, NC

Historical Features


Fort Macon, located on Bogue Banks, offers a rich blend of history and recreation. The fort was completed in 1834, where it played roles in the Spanish-American War, the Civil War, and World War II. Today, visitors can explore the fully restored fortifications, enjoy guided tours, and participate in live reenactments that bring military history to life. The surrounding state park also features beautiful beaches, picnic areas, and nature trails, making it a popular destination for both history buffs and nature lovers.

The Wright Brothers National Memorial stands as a testament to the ingenuity of the Wright Brothers, who conducted their experimental flights here. The visitor center houses replicas of the 1903 Wright Flyer and glider and exhibits detailing the brothers' experiments and achievements. A large monument atop Kill Devil Hill commemorates their historic flights, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape where aviation history was made.

Edenton serves as a living museum of colonial and early American architecture with over 70 historic landmarks. Visitors can take a trolley tour around the town to see sites such as the 1767 Chowan County Courthouse, one of the oldest courthouses in the country, and the Harriet Jacobs Trail sites, which tell the story of a runaway slave girl who became a noted abolitionist author.

Outdoor Activities


The Outer Banks is a top-tier vacation destination known for its wide, sandy beaches. The area is home to several famous lighthouses like Cape Hatteras Lighthouse, which visitors can climb for stunning ocean views. The Outer Banks is a haven for water sports fanatics, offering some of the best conditions for surfing, kiteboarding, and windsurfing on the East Coast.

Covering 160,000 acres, Croatan National Forest is notable for its coastal location, offering a mix of saltwater estuaries, bogs, and pocosin wetlands along with pine forests. It's an ideal spot for eco-tours, birdwatching, and spotting wildlife such as black bears and red-cockaded woodpeckers. The forest's diverse landscapes provide scenic backdrops for outdoor activities like hiking, mountain biking, and boating.

The Cape Lookout National Seashore is a secluded set of barrier islands that stretch for 56 miles and are accessible only by private ferry. It's perfect for those seeking a peaceful retreat with opportunities for fishing, shelling, and exploring the historic Cape Lookout Lighthouse. The seashore is also known for its wild horse populations, which roam freely on Shackleford Banks, offering a unique wildlife viewing experience.

Cultural Points


The North Carolina Maritime Museum is a museum in Beaufort that attracts history enthusiasts with its exhibits on coastal life, maritime heritage, and naval history. Important aspects of this museum include displays of the area's piracy, shipwrecks, and the significant fishing industry. The museum also serves as a research center with artifacts and materials recovered from underwater archaeological excavations, including those from the notorious pirate Blackbeard's ship.

Located on the grounds of the lost Roanoke Colony, Elizabethan Gardens is a living memorial to the first English settlers in America. This botanical garden features a spectacular collection of Renaissance statues, Elizabethan-style knot gardens, and a variety of plants that bloom throughout the seasons, creating a lush landscape that transports visitors back to the 16th century.

As the largest lagoon along the U.S. East Coast, Pamlico Sound plays a crucial role in both the local environment and economy. It offers some of the best sailing, windsurfing, and fishing spots in North Carolina. The sound's extensive waterways also support commercial and recreational fishing, making it a vital part of the region's maritime culture.
